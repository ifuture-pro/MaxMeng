English

China’s political development has been my research interest since I started my PhD study. In the past few years, I have been paying close attention to the institutional and technological aspects of Chinese political transformation in the digital era, with a particular focus on two strongly-related questions of political development in contemporary China: why and how the Chinese state and its elites respond to public opinion in a rigid top-down regime; and, conversely, how China’s bottom-up participation and state-society is shaped by ICT technologies, and the consequences of the widespread digital governance applications for both good governance and political control. In addition, I am also interested in exploring the cutting-edge political methodologies, such as computational social science, experiments, and large-scale online surveys.
